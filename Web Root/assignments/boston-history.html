Boston history

3 pages • Boston history, your POV • 15-20 hours

Overview

Write, draw, and letter three pages of comics continuity (minimum) about a Boston historical event. Your story must be thoroughly researched and told from your unique point of view. Provide historical and visual research, character notes, and setting details.

Read my Boston historical story

Why this assignment exists

For working adults: Historical comics require the same research discipline as journalism — you have to get the facts right before you can take creative liberties. This assignment trains you to work with primary sources and visual evidence, skills that apply directly to any non-fiction or documentary comics work.

For portfolio builders: Non-fiction and historical comics are a growing market. An assignment like this demonstrates range — that you can write, research, and draw something rooted in the real world, not just genre fantasy. It also shows editorial judgment: choosing what to include, what to leave out, and whose story to tell.

What you'll learn

  • Researching and verifying historical source material
  • Translating research into visual storytelling
  • Period-accurate rendering of fashion, technology, and setting
  • Developing a personal point of view on factual events
  • Managing dialogue density across a page
  • Full pipeline from event selection through production

Workflow

Phase 1: Event selection

Choose a Boston historical or current event that resonates with you. Come prepared to explain why this event matters to you personally — your point of view is as important as the facts.

Tasks
  • Identify your event
  • Articulate your point of view on it
  • Begin preliminary research
  • Get instructor sign-off before proceeding

Phase 2: Visual research and thumbnails

Gather visual and historical reference to support your story. Pay attention to relevant politics, unique social habits, fashion, technology, and pop culture details that ground the reader in the period.

Tasks
  • Research period fashion and technology
  • Gather visual reference for settings and characters
  • Develop character notes
  • Thumbnail all three pages
  • Get instructor sign-off before proceeding

Phase 3: Pencil art and lettering

Upon instructor feedback and sign-off, proceed to drawing on production paper. All research and tweaking should stop now. Focus on visual narrative, storytelling, camera angles, lettering style, and placement.

Tasks
  • Transfer thumbnails to production paper
  • Pencil all figures and backgrounds
  • Hand letter all dialogue and captions
  • Check balloon placement and reader flow
  • Get instructor sign-off before proceeding

Phase 4: Inking

Upon instructor feedback and sign-off, proceed to getting your final art ready for production!

Tasks
  • Ink all figures and backgrounds
  • Ink borders and panel gutters
  • Clean up and erase pencil lines
  • Get instructor sign-off before proceeding

Phase 5: Production

Upon instructor feedback and sign-off, proceed to format your pages per these Photoshop production instructions.

Tasks
  • Scan all pages at correct resolution
  • Follow Photoshop production instructions
  • Export final files in required format
  • Receive instructor sign-off

Requirements

  • Minimum page count: 3
  • Minimum balloons per page: 4 average
  • Art dimensions: 9 x 14.5 inches
  • Lettering: By hand
  • Ames guide: 3.5 scale, two-thirds ratio

Common challenges

  • Wikipedia-level research: Surface-level facts produce surface-level stories. Push past the obvious sources to find the detail that only you would notice.
  • Losing your POV: This isn't a textbook entry — it's a story told by you, about something that matters to you. The research should serve your perspective, not replace it.
  • Underwriting dialogue: The four-balloon minimum exists for a reason — historical comics especially need text to anchor the reader in time and place.
